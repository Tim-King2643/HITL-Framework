HITL Design Insight — I2M

2 Decision gates (80% Human at M3) sit with the Executive Steering Committee — portfolio governance and lifecycle management carry capital-allocation authority that stays irreducibly human, even though a parts/accessories variant of the portfolio decision routes to Product Management at lower stakes (documented as an exception rather than a parallel path). 4 Knowledge steps (65% Human at M3) span discovery research, concept generation, requirements definition, and design/prototyping — the largest cluster, and where AI augmentation of research synthesis and generative design tooling offers the clearest opportunity. Design/prototyping (2.3.1) also carries an inline Stage-Gate Committee approval — a real decision checkpoint with no ongoing work of its own, so it's marked on the step rather than given its own lane, and excluded from the aggregate H% math. 1 Document step (40% Human at M3) covers market-test synthesis and business-case finalization. 1 Transaction step (35% Human at M3) — production-readiness preparation — shows a smaller M1→M3 gap than a typical transactional step because mature PLM/MES systems already automate much of this work independent of any AI involvement. I2M aggregate at M3: 61.9% Human / 38.1% AI — reflecting a process where executive judgment and engineering/creative synthesis remain central even as AI absorbs more of the research and production-prep surface.
