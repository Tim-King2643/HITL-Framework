HITL Design Insight — I2R

Manage Customer Service Problems, Requests & Inquiries (6.2.2, 35% Human at M3) covers receiving customer problems, requests, and inquiries across channels, analyzing and resolving straightforward issues, responding to the customer, and identifying and routing upsell/cross-sell opportunities to sales. Classified as a Transaction process: high-volume, structured triage and response work, with mature service-desk automation already absorbing much of the routine load. Manage Customer Complaints (6.2.3, 82% Human at M3) covers receiving, routing, resolving, and responding to customer complaints, plus analyzing complaint patterns for redressal reporting. Classified as an Exception process: complaints carry reputational and relationship risk that keeps resolution authority irreducibly human, even with AI support for severity scoring and pattern detection. Process Warranty Claims (6.3.2, 80% Human at M3) covers receiving and validating the claim, investigating the issue including root-cause analysis, determining the responsible party, approving or rejecting the claim, and authorizing payment. Classified as a Decision process: the approve/reject determination is a real financial-authority decision that anchors the entire step at a high retained-human baseline. Service Products (6.3.4, 65% Human at M3) covers confirming customer service requirements, scheduling resources, executing the repair, and ensuring service quality through customer feedback. Classified as a Knowledge process: the actual repair and service-quality judgment is skilled technical work, even as scheduling and order administration are more automatable. I2R aggregate at M3: 65.5% Human / 34.5% AI — the highest retained-Human% of any module built so far, reflecting I2R's consequence-heavy character: complaint resolution and warranty-claim decisions carry reputational, financial, and relationship risk that keeps human judgment central even at full design maturity, with the smallest M1→M3 gap (-13.5pt) of any module to date.
